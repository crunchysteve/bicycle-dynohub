## Dynohub for Greenspeed Trikes

* NOTE:- This is only a proof of concept schematic, do not print this in any material, especially plastic, as it has not been designed to exact measurements and has no disk brake flange.

A very basic and unrefined proof-of-concept sketch for a Greenspeed dynamo hub. It's designed to use 6x KK-P10/10 electromagnets and 12x 10x3, 3mm drilled N35 rare earth magnets. Actual GT20 hubs have yet to be accurately measured, so the axle hole, hub flanges and spokes are yet to be accurately positiond, the bearing seats have no seating lips and the magnet clearances have yet to be finessed. 

<img width="2184" height="1738" alt="DynoHub" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/5f159dfd-3944-4e29-85ca-d807caeec38f" />

The [KK-P10/10 pot electromagnet](https://hsmagnets.blogspot.com/2017/04/round-powerful-mini-electromagnet-mk.html) comes in 6, 12 and 24v versions, able to be energised at up to 2 watts. The 6 volt version can deliver up to 333 mA, so 6 of these in a radial array, energised by 12, alternating, rotating rare-earth magnets could deliver as much as 12 watts per hub, assuming sufficient flux density from the magnets, although the 10mm x 3mm N35 magnets shown in the cutaway sketch, may not supply quite that much flux.

My plan is to first print a PLA, PETG or ABS prototype, simply to test the concept. If moderate rotations provide reasonable power outputs after losses, at least to a conventional dyno level, then, I'll measure up my stock Greenspeed hubs and start shaping the design for dimensional accuracy, increased strength and lighter weight, before trying a steel or aluminium prototype.
